Heart failure is a major cardiovascular condition associated with several risk factors such as obesity, hypertension, and metabolic disorders. Excess body fat contributes to chronic inflammation in the body and increases the likelihood of cardiovascular diseases.

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), millions of people worldwide suffer from heart failure, and the prevalence of obesity among these patients is high. Obesity is considered an important risk factor for the development of heart failure and other cardiovascular complications.

Several factors are associated with cardiovascular diseases, including high cholesterol, hypertension, sedentary lifestyle, diabetes, and metabolic disorders. These factors can damage blood vessels and affect the normal functioning of the heart.

Common symptoms of cardiovascular diseases include chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, dizziness, and palpitations. Early identification of these symptoms is important for diagnosis and treatment.

Understanding the risk factors and symptoms of cardiovascular diseases is essential for prevention strategies and for improving patient care and quality of life.
