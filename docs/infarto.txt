Heart attack (acute myocardial infarction) is one of the leading causes of death in Brazil. It occurs when blood flow to part of the heart muscle is suddenly blocked, usually due to the formation of a blood clot.

The most common symptom of a heart attack is pain or discomfort in the chest, which may spread to the back, face, or left arm. The pain is usually intense and may be accompanied by cold sweat, shortness of breath, paleness, and a feeling of fainting.

In elderly people, the main symptom may be shortness of breath instead of chest pain. In some cases, especially among diabetic patients, a heart attack may occur without clear symptoms.

Risk factors for heart attack include smoking, sedentary lifestyle, unhealthy diet, high cholesterol levels, stress, hypertension, and diabetes.

Treatment for heart attack requires immediate medical attention. Early identification of symptoms and rapid emergency response are essential to improve survival.

Prevention strategies include regular physical activity, healthy eating habits, avoiding tobacco use, and controlling cardiovascular risk factors.
