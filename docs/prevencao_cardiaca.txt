Cardiovascular diseases are among the leading causes of death worldwide. One of the most serious conditions is myocardial infarction (heart attack), which occurs when blood flow to part of the heart muscle is suddenly blocked.

Common symptoms of heart attack include chest pain or discomfort, pain that may spread to the arms, back, neck, or face, shortness of breath, sweating, paleness, and dizziness. In elderly people, shortness of breath may be the main symptom.

Several risk factors increase the probability of cardiovascular diseases, including smoking, sedentary lifestyle, unhealthy diet, obesity, high cholesterol, hypertension, and diabetes.

Regular physical activity can significantly reduce the risk of heart attack. Health experts recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate physical activity per week.

Healthy habits such as balanced nutrition, regular exercise, avoiding tobacco use, and controlling blood pressure and cholesterol levels are essential strategies for preventing cardiovascular diseases.
